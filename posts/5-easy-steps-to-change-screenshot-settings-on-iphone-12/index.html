In this article, I’ll provide you with five easy steps to quickly change screenshot settings on your iPhone 12.

First, open the settings app and scroll down to find the General option. Then tap on the Accessibility option. Once you’re on this page, you’ll see a ‘Screenshots’ option. Tap on it and you can specify whether you’d like screenshots to be taken with the physical Side button, or the Volume Up button.

Next, you can adjust the format of the screenshots. Open the Settings app again and scroll down to find the Camera option. Tap on it and select Photo Format. Here, you can set the image format to either JPEG or HEIF.

Also, you can choose to save screenshots to the Photos app or iCloud Drive. To do this, you need to open the Settings app once more and select iCloud. Now, scroll down to Photos and select ‘iCloud Photos’. Here, you can toggle on or off the ‘Screenshots’ option.

Next, you can adjust the quality of the screenshots. This is done by opening the Settings app and tapping on ‘Camera’. Here, you can select ‘File Quality’ and choose between ‘High’, ‘Medium’, or ‘Low’.

Finally, you can also adjust the length of time a screenshot will stay on your screen before automatically being dismissed. To do this, open the Settings app, tap on ‘Accessibility’, and select ‘Screenshot Timeout’. Here, you can specify a time between 1 to 30 seconds.

Following these five simple steps, you can now customize the screenshot settings on your iPhone 12 effortlessly. Most of all, you can now take screenshots in any format, adjust their quality, and set the timeout limit according to your preference. So, go ahead and enjoy taking screenshots on your iPhone 12!
