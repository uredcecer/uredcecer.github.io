2. Steps to Flush DNS Cache on Android Tablet#

Flushing the DNS cache on your Android tablet can help improve your web browsing experience by allowing you access to the most recent version of a web page. To clear your DNS cache, you’ll need to go into your device’s settings and then connect to your Wi-Fi network. Here are the steps to flush the DNS cache on your Android tablet:

  1. Go to the Settings of your Android tablet and tap “Wi-Fi.”
  2. Select the network you’re connected to, then tap “Modify Network.”
  3. Check the box for “Show Advanced Options.”
  4. Scroll down and tap “Static” under IP Settings.
  5. Now select “DNS 1” and enter “8.8.8.8” as the primary DNS.
  6. Select “DNS 2” and enter “8.8.4.4” as the secondary DNS.
  7. Finally, tap Save and restart your tablet.

When you’re done, your Android tablet’s DNS cache should be cleared and your web browsing performance should be improved. It’s also a good idea to flush your DNS cache periodically to ensure you’re always accessing the most up-to-date version of web pages.
