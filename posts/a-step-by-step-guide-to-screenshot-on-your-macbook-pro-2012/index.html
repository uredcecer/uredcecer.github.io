- Taking Screenshot on Your MacBook Pro#

Taking screenshots on your MacBook Pro 2012 can be quite simple with the right steps. The first step to take a screenshot is to press the ‘Command + Shift + 3’ keys on your keyboard all at once. This will take a picture of your entire screen and save it as a PNG file on your desktop. To capture only a portion of the screen, the ‘Command + Shift + 4’ keys should be pressed instead. After pressing the keys, a small crosshair will appear on the screen, which can be used to drag and select the area of the screen you want to capture. Once an area has been selected, the screenshot will be saved to the desktop automatically. If the user wants to save the screenshot to the clipboard instead, the ‘Control’ key should be held while selecting the area. 2. Taking Screenshots on Your MacBook Pro#

Taking screenshots on your MacBook Pro are quick and easy. All you need is a few simple keyboard shortcuts. Here’s how to take a screenshot on a 2012 MacBook Pro:

First, press Command-Shift-3 to capture the entire screen. You’ll hear a shutter sound, and your screenshot will appear on your desktop.

If you only want to capture a portion of your screen, press Command-Shift-4. Your cursor will change to plus sign. To select the area, click and drag over it. When you’re done, release the mouse button and your screenshot will save to your desktop.

If you want to capture an open window or dialog box alone, press Command-Shift-4 and then press the Spacebar. The cursor will change to a camera icon. Simply click on the window or box you want to capture and your screenshot will be ready.

Screenshots are a great way to capture anything quickly and easily on your MacBook Pro. With just a few simple keyboard shortcuts, you’ll be taking screenshots like a pro!
