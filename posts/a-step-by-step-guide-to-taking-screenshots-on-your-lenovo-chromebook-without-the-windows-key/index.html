1. Explanation of What a Screenshot Is#

A screenshot is an image of what is currently displayed on your screen. It is used to capture what is shown on any device’s display at a given moment. Taking a screenshot is very useful for saving pictures of error messages, recording game scores, or capturing a moment of video for later use. If you own a Lenovo Chromebook, there’s no need for the Windows key to take a screenshot. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to take screenshots with a Lenovo Chromebook.

First, press the Ctrl + “switch window” button. This is usually the button with the arrow pointing away from each other, and may have an image of a laptop monitor on it. This will take a screenshot of the entire screen, save the image to your device, and copy the URL of the screenshot to your clipboard.

Next, press the Ctrl + Shift + “switch window” buttons to take a screenshot of a specific area. This will allow you to click and drag to select the area you want to take a screenshot of. Once you’ve selected the area you want, a screenshot will be taken, saved to your device, and the URL of the screenshot will be copied to your clipboard.

Finally, press the Ctrl + Alt + “switch window” buttons to take a screenshot of one window only. This will open a small dialogue box that will let you select which window you want to take a screenshot of. Once you select the window, a screenshot will be taken, saved to your device, and the URL of the screenshot will be copied to your clipboard.

With just three simple steps, you can take screenshots of your Lenovo Chromebook screen without the need for the Windows key. With this step-by-step guide, you can capture any moment displayed on your device in just a few button presses.

2. How to Take a Screenshot Without the Windows Key on a Lenovo Chromebook#

Taking a screenshot is a useful way to capture something on your screen so that you can easily save or share it. But it can be a bit challenging to figure out how to take a screenshot without the Windows key on a Lenovo Chromebook. Luckily, taking screenshots on a Lenovo Chromebook is still possible, and this step-by-step guide will show you how.

Firstly, press the CTRL + Overview shortcut key combination to capture the entire screen and save it your Downloads folder. This method will work on other Chromebooks as well, but note that the shortcut key combination may differ depending on the model.

Alternatively, press the CTRL + Shift + Switch window shortcut key combination to capture the current window you have open and save it in your Downloads folder. If done correctly, you should hear a shutter click sound and a notification should appear briefly at the bottom right corner of your screen.

Lastly, press the CTRL + Shift + Show all windows shortcut key combination to take a screenshot of multiple windows at the same time and save it into your Downloads folder. Note that this shortcut key combination will only work on Chromebooks with extended memory.

Now that you know how to take a screenshot on your Lenovo Chromebook without the Windows key, you can quickly capture something interesting on your screen and follow up on it later.
