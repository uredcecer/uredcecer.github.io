Q1: What is a screen capture?

A1: A screen capture, also known as a screenshot, is an image taken of what is currently displayed on a computer monitor or mobile device screen. It can be used to capture a static image of what is visible on the display, as well as to capture text or images that cannot be saved any other way.

Q2: What is the quickest way to screen capture on iPhone 13 Pro Max?

A2: iPhone 13 Pro Max has a built-in screen capture feature. To quickly capture a screenshot, simultaneously press and hold the Side button and Volume Up button. Once you release the buttons, a preview of your screenshot will appear in the bottom-left corner of your screen. To access the screenshot, tap on the preview and it will open in a new window.

Q3: Is there a way to screen capture a specific area of the iPhone 13 Pro Max display?

A3: Yes, you can capture a specific area of the display by using the iPhone 13 Pro Max built-in Markup feature. To do this, take a screenshot and tap on the preview. This will open the Markup tool, which allows you to select a portion of the screen you’d like to capture. Once you’ve selected the area, tap on the Done button and the screenshot will be saved.

Q4: Can I edit the screenshot before I save it?

A4: Yes, you can edit the screenshot before saving it. After taking the screenshot, tap on the preview and it will open in the Markup tool. This feature allows you to draw, add text, crop, and zoom in/out of the screenshot. Once you’ve made the appropriate edits, tap on the Done button and the screenshot will be saved to your device.

Q5: Where can I find the screenshots I have taken on my iPhone 13 Pro Max?

A5: The screenshots you’ve taken can be found in the Photos app on your iPhone 13 Pro Max. To access them, open the app and navigate to the “Screenshots” album located in the main menu. Here, you can view all the screenshots you’ve taken on your device.
