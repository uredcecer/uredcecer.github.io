1. Download the Snipping Tool#

To take a screenshot on a Microsoft Surface 10 device, there are 3 simple steps to follow. First, download the Snipping Tool from the Windows store. The Snipping Tool is a free-to-use app that allows users to take screenshots of their Microsoft Surface 10 screens. Second, launch the Snipping Tool and click “New”. This will open a crosshair-shaped cursor on your screen, which you can use to select the area you want to screenshot. Finally, once you’ve selected the desired area, click “Save” to save the screenshot in your device. The screenshot will then be saved to the Pictures folder. With these 3 simple steps, taking a screenshot on your Microsoft Surface 10 device is easy and quick.

A. Download the Snipping Tool#

Taking a screenshot on Microsoft Surface 10 is a breeze thanks to the pre-installed Snipping Tool. To get started, open the Start menu and search for the Snipping Tool. Once you have it open, select New from the top menu and begin to select the region of the screen you would like to take a screenshot of. 2. Select a Screenshot Mode#

Taking a screenshot on a Microsoft Surface 10 is easy! Follow these simple steps to get the perfect shot:

  1. Press the Windows logo and Volume Down buttons simultaneously. This will alert you with a shutter sound, and the screenshot will automatically save to your photo album.

  2. Select a Screenshot Mode. Pressing the same buttons again will provide an option for users to choose between a full screen or partial screenshot.

  3. Crop and Edit. After you’ve taken the screenshot, you can crop it and edit it via the Pen button on the tablet’s taskbar. This will open the screenshot in the drawing app, where you can crop and edit as desired.

  4. Share. After you’re finished editing, you can choose to share the screenshot via social media, email, or text message. You can also save it to the cloud to make sure you don’t lose it.

1. Setting up a Screenshot Mode#

Taking a screenshot on your Microsoft Surface 10 is an easy process when you know how to do it. Firstly, open the Start menu and select the Settings option. This will open the Windows settings screen. Click on the Ease of Access tab and select the Keyboard option. In the Keyboard Settings, select the Print Screen button from the list of available keys and then click the Add button at the bottom of the list. In the pop-up menu, select Screen Clipping. This will set the screenshot mode to Screen Clipping so you can take a screenshot of your Microsoft Surface 10. Finally, click the “Save” button to save the screenshot to your device.

2. Taking a Screenshot#

Taking screenshots on your Microsoft Surface 10 is quick and easy with the Snipping Tool. First, open the Snipping Tool by searching for the tool through the task bar. Then select a screenshot mode of your choice. You can choose from a Fullscreen Snip, Window Snip, Rectangular Snip or Free-form Snip. After selecting the screenshot mode, drag the cursor to capture the content of your screenshot. Once you have made the selection of the content you want, you will be taken to a new editing window where you can crop, highlight and add annotations to the screenshot if desired. Finally, save the screenshot to the location of your preference. With these easy steps, you are well on your way to taking a screenshot on your Microsoft Surface 10.

3. Save the Screenshot#

Taking a screenshot on Microsoft Surface 10 is simple and easy to do. To begin, simply press and hold down the ‘Windows’ and ‘Volume Down’ button simultaneously. This will take a snapshot of whatever is currently displayed on the screen. Next, you will see an image of the screenshot in the lower right of the screen. After that, press and hold the ‘Windows’ and ‘Volume Up’ button for a few seconds until a notification sound confirms the screenshot is successfully taken. Lastly, hit the ‘Save’ button or ‘Share’ button so you can immediately share the screenshot or store it safely in the photo gallery. With these three steps, you can easily take a screenshot on Microsoft Surface 10.

2. Take the Screenshot#

This will save the screenshot in the Screenshots folder in your Pictures library. To access this folder, tap the File Explorer button on the taskbar. Once you’re in the folder, you can easily share or upload the screenshot as you like. Be sure to save any screenshots you take, as they will be deleted if you snap another one without saving.
