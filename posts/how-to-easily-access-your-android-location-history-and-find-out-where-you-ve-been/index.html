1. What Is Android Location History#

Android Location History is a feature built into the Android OS that keeps track of all the places you’ve been to while using your device. This feature is enabled by default on most devices, and it stores your approximate location as often as possible. It can be particularly useful for business owners who need to keep track of their employees’ movements and for users who need to know where they’ve been. Accessing your location history is easy and can be done in just a few steps.

First, open your Google Settings app and navigate to the Security & location option. Here you can scroll down to the Location section and tap the Location History entry. This will bring up your Location History, divided into day-of-week categories. Tap on each of these to see a timeline of your locations for that day.

If you want to see more detailed information about each location, tap on it. You’ll get a Google Maps timeline view, complete with the time and date of your visit and an approximate location. You can also take a look at the timeline to see how long you’ve stayed in each location.

You can also access this information from the Google Maps app. Open the app, tap the hamburger menu icon and select Location History from the left sidebar. From here you’ll see the same detailed timeline featured in the Google Settings app.

Finally, you can also add to your Location History by manually adding place points. Simply tap the ‘+’ icon at the bottom-right corner of the screen and select a place from the list. This way, you’ll be able to store a place you’ve already visited or plan to visit in the future.
